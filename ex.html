Unit: 3 - Nutrition and Safe Food

Exercise

A. Very Short Answers

a. Define nutrients.
Nutrients are useful chemical substances found in food that help our body to grow.

b. Classify nutrients into two broad types.
Two broad types of nutrients are minerals and vitamins.

c. What do you mean by proper nutrition?
Proper nutrition means having the right amount of nutrients for body size, work, and age.

d. Write one major function of protein.
Protein helps to build, repair, and replace body tissues and damaged cells.

e. List any two foods rich in Vitamin A.
Carrot and papaya are rich sources of Vitamin A.

f. What is the function of Vitamin B?
Vitamin B helps in energy production and proper brain function.

g. What nutrient do you get from citrus fruits?
Citrus fruits give us Vitamin C which prevents scurvy.

h. List some minerals our body needs.
Our body needs calcium, iron, iodine, and potassium.

i. How are some food considered "junk food"?
Foods with low nutrition and high fat, sugar, or salt are called junk food.

j. Define food adulteration in a sentence.
Mixing harmful or low-quality substances into food is called food adulteration.

B. Short Answers

a. Classify nutrients based on their functions.
Nutrients are classified as energy-giving (carbohydrates, fats), body-building (proteins), and protective (vitamins, minerals).

b. What is the function of carbohydrates? List two.
Carbohydrates give energy, maintain body temperature, and help us perform physical and mental work effectively.

c. Why avoid too much rice and sweet foods?
Too much rice and sweet food causes obesity, diabetes, and tooth problems due to excess sugar.

d. Why are fats not very healthy? Should we eat them?
Fats are unhealthy if eaten too much, but needed in small amounts for energy and warmth.

e. How are vitamins divided? Give examples.
Vitamins are fat-soluble (Vitamin A, D) and water-soluble (Vitamin B, C), both essential for body.

f. “You get Vitamin D from sunlight.” Is this true?
Yes, our skin produces Vitamin D when exposed to sunlight. It's needed for strong bones.

g. Name two sources of Vitamin E and K.
Vitamin E: sunflower oil and nuts. Vitamin K: spinach and cabbage.

h. Why are minerals necessary? Give two reasons.
Minerals like calcium and iron are needed for strong bones, healthy blood, and proper body functions.

i. Give two effects of chemically grown food.
Chemicals in food can damage organs and may cause diseases like cancer or allergies.

j. What do you mean by junk food? Give examples.
Junk food has low nutrition and high calories. Examples: noodles and soft drinks.

k. Two effects of junk food on our body.
Junk food can cause obesity, high blood pressure, and increase risk of heart problems.

l. Why does chemically treated food affect health?
Chemically treated food may contain harmful substances that damage internal organs and cause illness.

m. Two ways to eat healthy food at school.
I can carry homemade lunch and avoid buying oily, packed, or colored food from canteens.

n. Preventive measures for food adulteration.
Buy food from trusted shops, check expiry date, and avoid cheap, unusually colored, or mixed items.
