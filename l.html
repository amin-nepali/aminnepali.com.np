Unit: 3 - Nutrition and Safe Food

C. Long Answer Questions (More than 50 words)

a. What are some healthy food? Give examples. If you were to eat the foods that you have listed, would you be healthy? Why or why not?
Healthy foods are those that provide nutrients without harmful effects. Some examples are fresh fruits, green vegetables, milk, eggs, rice, lentils, and meat. These foods give us energy, protect us from diseases, and help in the growth of our body. Yes, if we eat these regularly, we will stay fit, active, and healthy because our body will get all the essential nutrients it needs.

b. List down the types of Vitamin B. Also mention their individual functions.
There are many types of Vitamin B. Vitamin B1 helps in energy production. B2 keeps our skin and eyes healthy. B3 helps in digestion and keeping the skin clear. B5 is important for hormone making. B6 supports brain function. B12 is needed to form red blood cells and keeps our nerves healthy. All these B vitamins help our body to stay active and function well.

c. In this day and age most foods are grown using chemicals. “Organic foods” are expensive, so most people can’t afford them. Taking this into consideration, suggest four ways to eat healthy food.
It is true that organic foods are costly, but we can still eat healthy by following some simple ways. First, always wash fruits and vegetables properly before eating or cooking. Second, avoid street foods and oily snacks. Third, grow some vegetables at home if possible. Fourth, check labels and expiry dates while buying packed foods. These habits will help us stay safe and healthy.

d. “It is better to eat home-made foods at school than many of those sold in local shops.” Elaborate this statement with examples.
Home-made food is more hygienic, nutritious, and fresh. Food sold in shops near school like packaged noodles, colored juices, and chips may be stale, oily, and harmful for our health. Home food like roti, fruits, rice, vegetables, and boiled eggs keeps us energetic and focused. It also helps avoid junk and saves money. So, eating home-made food is a better and safer option for students.

e. Food adulteration is a big problem nowadays. Mention a couple of real examples of food adulteration. How is this a problem for us? Give any two reasons.
Food adulteration means mixing harmful or low-quality materials in food items. For example, adding water to milk or mixing soap in ghee. This makes food unsafe and low in quality. It can cause food poisoning, long-term diseases, and damage organs. Also, it is cheating and affects public health badly. We must be careful and avoid buying food from unknown and cheap sources.
