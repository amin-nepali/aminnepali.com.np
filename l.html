Unit: 3 – Nutrition and Safe Food

Write Long Answers (Approx. 30 Words)

a. What are some healthy food? Give examples. If you were to eat the foods that you have listed, would you be healthy? Why or Why not?
Healthy foods include rice, vegetables, fruits, milk, and eggs. Eating them regularly keeps the body strong, prevents disease, and provides all nutrients needed for growth and proper functioning of the body.

b. List down the types of Vitamin B. Also mention their individual functions.
Vitamin B1 gives energy, B2 protects eyes, B3 helps digestion, B5 makes hormones, B6 supports the brain, and B12 helps blood formation. All B vitamins are essential for good health.

c. In this day and age most foods are grown using chemicals. “Organic foods” are expensive, so most people can’t afford them. Taking this into consideration, suggest four ways to eat healthy food.
Wash fruits and vegetables well, avoid oily junk food, buy fresh local food, and grow vegetables at home. These simple practices help us eat healthier even without expensive organic foods.

d. “It is better to eat home-made foods at school than many of those sold in local shops.” Elaborate this statement with examples.
Home-made food is fresh and safe. School shops often sell stale, oily items like noodles and chips. Foods from home like fruits, roti, and boiled eggs are healthier and more hygienic.

e. Food adulteration is a big problem nowadays. Mention a couple of real examples of food adulteration. How is this a problem for us? Give any two reasons.
Water is mixed in milk, soap in ghee. Such adulterated food is harmful, causes serious illness, reduces nutrition, and may lead to diseases like cancer and stomach problems. It’s dangerous.
